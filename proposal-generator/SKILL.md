# Proposal Generator

> Generate a professional service proposal in 60 seconds from client name, industry, and pain points.

## Description

Proposal Generator creates polished, ready-to-send service proposals in markdown format. Input the client details and pain points; output a complete proposal with scope, timeline, pricing structure, and terms. Stop spending 2 hours writing proposals that should take 2 minutes.

## Instructions

When generating a proposal:

1. **Ask for missing info** if not provided: client name, industry, pain points, budget range, timeline expectations.

2. **Mirror their language**: If they said "we're drowning in admin work," the proposal says "eliminate the admin bottleneck" — not "optimize operational efficiency."

3. **Three pricing tiers**: Always present 3 options. The middle option should be the one you want them to pick (anchoring effect). The top option makes the middle look reasonable.

4. **Quantify the ROI**: Every proposal should include at least one line showing: "This costs $X. It saves/generates $Y. Payback period: Z weeks."

5. **Urgency without pressure**: The 14-day validity creates natural urgency. Don't add fake scarcity.

## Example

### Input
> Client: GreenTree Landscaping (Mike, owner). Industry: Landscaping/home services. Pain points: Taking 4+ hours to respond to leads, losing bids to faster competitors, admin work eating 15 hours/week. Budget: $2-5K. Timeline: wants it running in 2 weeks.

### Output
*(Full proposal generated using the template above, with GreenTree-specific language, 3 pricing tiers at $2,500 / $4,000 / $1,500/mo, and ROI showing 3 recovered leads/month = $18K additional annual revenue.)*

## Customization

- **Agency model**: Add "Ongoing Management" section with monthly retainer details
- **Product/SaaS**: Replace phases with "Onboarding → Configuration → Launch"
- **Consulting**: Add "Methodology" section describing your framework
- **One-time project**: Remove retainer option, add maintenance upsell

## Pro Tips

- Send as PDF, not a link. PDFs feel more official and don't require login.
- Follow up the proposal with a 60-second Loom video walking through it. Proposals with video close 35% higher.
- If they ask for a discount, remove scope instead of lowering price. "We can hit that budget — here's what that version looks like."
