# Daily Brief Generator

> Generate a structured daily business briefing from any data sources in under 60 seconds.

## Description

Daily Brief Generator produces a morning briefing that tells you exactly what matters today: revenue status, pipeline movement, content performance, team tasks, and fires to put out. It pulls from whatever data sources you have — CRM exports, spreadsheets, dashboards, or raw notes — and outputs a scannable brief you can read in 2 minutes.

No more starting your day checking 7 different tabs. One brief. Everything that matters. Nothing that doesn't.

## Activation

This skill activates when:
- The user asks for a morning briefing or daily brief
- The user wants a summary of their business status
- The user provides business data and asks "what should I focus on today?"
- The user mentions daily standup, morning routine, or business dashboard

**Trigger phrases**: "morning brief", "daily briefing", "what's happening today", "business summary", "daily standup", "start my day", "what should I focus on"

## Instructions

When asked to generate a brief, follow this process:

1. **Identify available data**: Ask what sources exist (CRM, spreadsheet, notes, previous briefs). If none provided, generate the template with placeholder prompts.

2. **Extract signals**: From the provided data, identify:
   - Revenue numbers and trajectory
   - Anything time-sensitive or at risk
   - Pipeline changes in the last 24-48 hours
   - Scheduled commitments

3. **Prioritize ruthlessly**: The TOP 3 PRIORITIES section is the most important. Apply this filter:
   - Does it generate revenue today? (Priority 1)
   - Does it prevent revenue loss? (Priority 2)
   - Does it create future revenue? (Priority 3)
   - Everything else is noise.

4. **Flag fires**: Anything that will cost money, lose a client, or miss a deadline if not handled TODAY goes in FIRES. Be specific about the action needed, not just the problem.

5. **Keep it scannable**: The entire brief should be readable in under 2 minutes. No paragraphs. Bullets and numbers only.

## Example

### Input
> I have 3 clients: Bryson ($4k/mo, content pipeline running, day 13 of eval), TrueBrand (owes me $2,045, invoice sent March 1), and a new lead Mike from GreenTree who wants to meet this week. Yesterday I published 2 Instagram posts for Bryson, one got 340 likes. I have $2,000 MTD revenue, target is $8,000. No calls scheduled today.

### Output

# Daily Brief — March 26, 2026
## Generated at 8:00 AM

---

### REVENUE SNAPSHOT
- **MTD Revenue**: $2,000 / $8,000 target (25%) -- behind pace
- **Pipeline Value**: $6,045 ($4,000 Bryson renewal + $2,045 TrueBrand AR)
- **At Risk**: TrueBrand invoice 25 days overdue
- **Closing This Week**: GreenTree (new lead, meeting TBD)

### FIRES
1. **TrueBrand $2,045 overdue** — 25 days past due. Send final notice today with 48-hour deadline. Escalate to phone call if no response by noon.
2. **GreenTree meeting not scheduled** — Mike expressed urgency. Book the call TODAY before he cools off or finds someone else.

### TOP 3 PRIORITIES
1. **Book GreenTree call** — Why: New revenue opportunity, inbound lead with high intent. Fastest path to closing the $6K MTD gap.
2. **Collect TrueBrand payment** — Why: $2,045 sitting in AR. Phone call > email at this stage.
3. **Bryson content: double down on what's working** — Why: 340 likes signals a winning format. Produce 2 more in that style today.

### PIPELINE MOVEMENT (Last 24h)
- New leads: GreenTree (Mike, owner, ~$800K revenue, needs admin automation)
- Moved forward: None
- Gone cold: TrueBrand (no payment response)
- Lost: None

### CONTENT STATUS
- Published yesterday: 2 IG posts for Bryson (1 hit: 340 likes)
- Scheduled today: Check Bryson content queue
- Performance: The 340-like post is 3x above average — analyze what made it work

### CLIENT HEALTH
- Bryson: GREEN — Day 13 of 60-90 eval. Pipeline running. 340-like post is a win.
- TrueBrand: RED — Overdue payment. Relationship at risk.

### CALENDAR
- No calls scheduled. **Fix this.** Book GreenTree and a TrueBrand follow-up.

### YESTERDAY'S SCORECARD
- Content published: 2/2
- Revenue collected: $0
- New leads: 1

---

## Customization

Adjust the brief sections based on your business:

- **Solo operator**: Remove "Team Tasks", add "Energy/Focus Check"
- **Agency**: Add "Deliverables Due" section with client deadlines
- **SaaS**: Add "Churn Signals" and "Feature Requests" sections
- **Sales team**: Add "Rep Leaderboard" and "Call Recordings to Review"

The template is modular — add or remove sections to match your operating rhythm.
